## 1. Emergence
1. **What is the best definition of emergence in Multi-Agent Systems?**
   - A) Complex macro-level patterns arising from simple micro-level interactions.
   - B) The ability of an agent to emerge from a hidden state.
   - C) A centralized control mechanism that dictates system behavior.
   - D) The programmed output of a complex equation.
   **Answer: A**
2. **Which of the following is a classic example of emergence?**

   - B) A flock of birds maneuvering in coordination.
   - C) An assembly line robot building a car.

   - C) They involve a single super-agent that controls everything.
   - D) They eliminate the need for computer simulation.
   **Answer: B**
4. **Emergent phenomena are typically:**
   - A) Easily predictable by looking at a single agent.
   - B) Linear and additive.
   - C) Non-linear and often surprising.
   - D) Dictated by a top-down leader.
   **Answer: C**
5. **In the context of traffic, a traffic jam forming without an accident is:**
   - A) A coding error.
   - B) An emergent phenomenon.
   - C) A result of centralized planning.
   - D) Impossible in ABMs.
   **Answer: B**
6. **Which statement is TRUE about emergence?**
   - A) It requires complex agents to produce complex behavior.
   - B) Simple rules can lead to complex system behavior.
   - C) Emergence only happens in biological systems.
   - D) Emergence implies intention on the part of the agents.
   **Answer: B**
